Why Headlights Turn Yellow

Headlight lenses are polycarbonate plastic, and years of UV exposure break down their factory coating — the result is the cloudy, yellowed haze you see on so many vehicles. It doesn't just look bad: hazy lenses scatter your headlight beam and can seriously cut down night visibility.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Is headlight restoration permanent?

We finish every restoration with UV protection to slow re-oxidation, but no restoration is forever — sun exposure will eventually haze any polycarbonate lens. Garaged vehicles hold up much longer.

Can it be done as part of my detail?

Yes — it's an add-on we can fold into any detail appointment. Just mention it when you request your quote.

How much does it cost?

It's priced as an add-on based on lens condition. Include your headlights in your quote photos and we'll give you an exact number.

Is it really better than replacing the headlights?

For oxidized-but-intact lenses, yes — restoration delivers most of the clarity at a fraction of the cost. Cracked or internally damaged housings need replacement, and we'll tell you honestly if that's the case.
