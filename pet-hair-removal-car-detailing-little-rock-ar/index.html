Why Pet Hair Is So Hard to Remove

Pet hair doesn't just sit on your carpet — it corkscrews into the fiber weave and locks in with static. That's why a shop vac barely touches it. Getting it out takes purpose-made tools: rubber blades, specialty brushes, compressed air, and patient, methodical passes over every panel of fabric.

How We Tackle It

  • Seats, seat rails, and seams — where hair hides deepest
  • Carpet and floor mats, worked section by section
  • Cargo areas and trunk — the worst zone in most dog vehicles
  • Followed by a full vacuum and interior wipe-down

Pet hair removal pairs naturally with an interior detail or full detail — most customers bundle them.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you get all the pet hair out?

The vast majority, yes. Deeply woven hair in loose-weave carpet can take multiple passes, and we'll tell you up front if any area will be stubborn.

Is pet hair removal extra?

Yes — it's an add-on priced by how much hair there is, since heavy hair takes real time and specialized tools. Photos let us quote it accurately.

Which vehicles are the worst for pet hair?

SUV cargo areas and truck back seats, by far. If that's your situation, send photos of those zones specifically.

How do I keep it from coming back?

A washable cargo liner or seat cover is the single best investment a dog owner can make — we're happy to point you in the right direction after your detail.
