Detailing a Bike the Right Way

Motorcycles concentrate everything that's hard about detailing into a small package: paint, chrome, matte finishes, leather, and exposed electrics all inches apart. That's why we work by hand — low-pressure washing, panel-by-panel drying, and product matched to each surface.

  • Careful hand wash — no high-pressure blasts at bearings or electronics
  • Chrome and trim polished
  • Paint protected; matte finishes treated correctly
  • Wheels, spokes, and hard-to-reach spots detailed

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Is your process safe for motorcycles?

Yes — that's the point of doing it by hand. Low-pressure washing, careful drying, and no blasting water at bearings, chains, or electronics.

Do you handle matte finishes?

We do — matte paint needs dedicated products (no polish, no gloss dressings), and we treat it accordingly.

Can you detail my bike and my car in one visit?

Yes, and it's the most efficient way to book. Mention both vehicles when you request your quote.

How much does motorcycle detailing cost?

It's quoted per bike based on condition. Text photos and we'll give you a straight answer.
