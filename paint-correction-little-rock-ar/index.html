What Paint Correction Fixes

Years of automatic car washes, dusty towels, and sun exposure leave paint covered in fine scratches that scatter light — that's why older paint looks dull even when it's clean. Paint correction uses machine polishing to level those defects:

  • Swirl marks and wash marring
  • Light scratches that haven't broken through the clear coat
  • Oxidation and dullness
  • Water spot etching and holograms

Honest expectations: scratches deep enough to catch a fingernail have usually gone through the clear coat and can't be polished out — we'll tell you what's fixable before we start.

Polishing vs. Ceramic Coating

They solve different problems. Correction fixes the defects already in your paint. A ceramic coating protects the finish going forward. They pair perfectly: correct the paint to its best possible condition, then coat it to lock that finish in for years.

Correction is quoted by vehicle size and paint condition — send photos in good light and we'll tell you exactly what your paint needs.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you remove every scratch?

No honest detailer can promise that. Swirls and light scratches polish out beautifully; anything deep enough to catch a fingernail has likely gone through the clear coat and may only improve, not disappear.

How long does paint correction take?

Anywhere from a few hours to a full day depending on paint condition and the level of correction you choose.

Should I do paint correction before ceramic coating?

If your paint has visible swirls or dullness, yes — the coating locks in whatever's underneath, so correcting first gives you a dramatically better final result.

How much does it cost?

It's quoted per vehicle based on size and paint condition. Text photos of your paint in sunlight and we'll give you a straight answer.
